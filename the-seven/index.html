John, better known as the Homelander, is the main antagonist of the Amazon series The Boys. He also appears as a major antagonist in its animated spin-off series The Boys Presents: Diabolical and one of the two overarching antagonists, alongside Victoria Neuman, of the spin-off series Gen V. Homelander is the former leader of The Seven, the strongest Supe in the world, and the archenemy of Billy Butcher and The Boys. With the face of a movie star and the powers of a god, Homelander is considered the greatest superhero alive. Not only can he fly, but he possesses super strength and super durability far beyond the capacity of other superheroes, super senses (sight, hearing, etc.), X-ray vision, and laser vision.

The Deep is a long-time member of the Seven, and known to the public as the "King of the Seas". The Deep is the most mature, civilized member of the team and often bears the brunt of other characters' contempt, disregard, and racism. Occasionally finding himself in embarrassing happenstance that steal his metaphorical thunder, such as arriving at The Boys Flatiron HQ to confront them and Butcher slamming the door in his face after telling him to leave.

Black Noir is the main antagonist of the Dynamite Entertainment adult comic book series The Boys, and the posthumous overarching antagonist of its sequel/spin-off miniseries The Boys: Dear Becky. He was a silent and emotionless and also a sadomasochistic member of the Seven. He served as the secondary antagonist until it was revealed that he was the true mastermind of the rape of Billy Butcher's wife, Becky, and the sexual assault on Hughie's girlfriend, Starlight, as also the true archenemy of Billy Butcher.

Jessica Bradley, better known as Sister Sage, is a major character and former antagonist of the Amazon series The Boys. She also appears as the secondary antagonist of Season Two of its spin-off series Gen V. She is a Supe from Detroit, Michigan who is known for being the smartest person on the planet. She was recruited by Homelander to join The Seven and became the CEO of Vought International. Intending to finally be able to read her books in peace, Sister Sage began to execute a master plan related to the Godolkin Virus to indirectly cause a war between humans and Supes, which led her to turn against Homelander and join The Boys to kill him.

Misty Tucker Gray, better known as Firecracker, is a major antagonist of the Amazon series The Boys. She also appears as a minor antagonist in Season Two of its spin-off series Gen V. She is a self-promoting exhibitionist and a hated rival of Starlight, whom she despises for insulting her in a show during her childhood and seeks revenge on her. Eventually, Firecracker entered The Seven and sought to form a sexual relationship with Homelander, although she also had an affair with his father Soldier Boy.

Clara Vought[1] (née Risinger), better known as Stormfront, is a major antagonist of the Amazon series The Boys. She is also one of the two protagonists of its prequel spin-off Vought Rising, alongside Soldier Boy. She is a superhero who replaced Translucent as a member of The Seven. Stormfront joined the Seven after Translucent's death became public and was briefly a rival to Homelander before the two became lovers. She is adept at using social media and has a large following online. Though she hid it from the public, Stormfront was a Nazi who used her position as a member of the Seven to promote her white supremacist agenda. She took pleasure in killing anybody who was not white, which is demonstrated when she massacres an entire block of apartments containing black residents while chasing Kenji Miyashiro, referring to him as a "yellow bastard".

Reggie Franklin,[2] better known as A-Train, is a major character and former antagonist of the Amazon series The Boys. He also appears as a minor character in its spin-off series Gen V. He was a speedster who used to be a part of the team known as the Teenage Kix, and is a former member of The Seven. He inadvertently killed Robin Ward, girlfriend of Hughie Campbell, with his powers while high on Compound V. This action led to Hughie becoming his archenemy, ultimately leading him to join The Boys. A-Train was let go from The Seven, but has since been asked to rejoin, and is the "fastest man in the world". Underneath the cavalier exterior of a celebrity superhero lies the soul of a mortal athlete, with a deep insecurity about staying at the top. He'll do whatever it takes to remain in The Seven, because he knows it's only a matter of time until a younger, faster challenger takes his place — and that's a problem even the fastest man in the world can't outrun.
